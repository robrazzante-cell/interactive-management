About Collective Intelligence

Collective Intelligence is a methodology that harnesses the wisdom and insights of groups to solve complex problems and make better decisions together.

Methodology Development: The Collective Intelligence research methodology (formally called Interactive Management Research) was developed by John Warfield and adapted by researchers and practitioners in organizational communication.

How it helps achieve organizational goals:

  • Diverse Perspectives: Brings together multiple viewpoints to identify comprehensive solutions
  • Structured Analysis: Uses systematic methods like ISM to organize and prioritize ideas
  • Democratic Participation: Ensures all voices are heard and valued in the decision-making process
  • Evidence-Based Results: Generates data-driven insights that guide strategic actions

Collective Intelligence Methodology

Interactive Management Research uses an open-source computer-based algorithm, Interpretive Structural Modeling (ISM; Warfield, 1976), for mapping complex relationships between ideas and factors.

The Modified Collective Intelligence Methodology:

  • Step 1: Idea Generation - Participants share ideas and clarifications in response to a guiding question (e.g., which human and organizational characteristics build a culture of innovation?)
  • Step 2: Coding & Factors - Ideas are analyzed and grouped into key factors (themes)
  • Step 3: Rank-Order Factors - Factors are then rank-ordered to identify the top 8-15 factors
  • Step 4: Structuring Factors - Participants use ISM to vote on the relationships between the factors, creating a visual map that shows which factors drive others according to the perceptions of the individual participant or group of stakeholders
  • Step 5: Analysis - Results guide decision-making and strategic planning

What to expect in ISM Voting:

  • You'll review a pair of factors and decide if one influences the other ("Does Factor A significantly support Factor B?")
  • Voting is simple: Vote using a simple Likert scale from 1-5
  • Your input creates a visual "map" of factor relationships
  • The process typically takes 20-30 minutes if done individually and up to several days if done in a group

Want to practice?
We encourage you to participate in one of our Public ISM Projects (go to the Public Projects tab) to get comfortable with the voting process before your main project begins!
